French Toast Recipe



Description

French toast is a dish made of sliced bread soaked in beaten eggs and typically milk, then pan fried. Alternative names and variants include "eggy bread", "Bombay toast", "gypsy toast", and "poor knights".

When French toast is served as a sweet dish, milk, sugar, vanilla or cinnamon are also commonly added before pan-frying, and then it may be topped with sugar (often powdered sugar), butter, fruit, or syrup. When it is a savory dish, it is generally fried with a pinch of salt or pepper, and it can then be served with a sauce such as ketchup or mayonnaise.

Ingredients

  • 3 slices of white bread
  • 1 egg
  • 1/4 cup of milk
  • 1/4 teaspoon of ground cinnamon (optional)
  • 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract (optional)
  • 1 tablespoon of butter, or more as needed
  • 1 tablespoon of syrup or honey to top with, or more to taste (optional)

Steps

  1. In a plate or shallow bowl, whisk together egg and milk (and optional ground cinnamon and vanilla extract).
  2. Lightly butter a griddle or pan over medium-high heat.
  3. Dunk a slice a bread into the egg mixture and coat both sides with the egg mixture. Let the bread soak in the mixture for a few seconds.
  4. Transfer soaked bread into the hot pan and cook until golden, about 3 to 4 minutes.
  5. When one side is golden, flip the bread and let the other side cook until golden.
  6. Transfer to a clean plate. Serve hot and top with butter and syrup.
  7. Other toppings besides syrup and butter include: powdered sugar; fresh raspberries or blueberries; slices of banana; Nutella; or bacon.
